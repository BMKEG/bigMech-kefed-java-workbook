Workbook for Big Mechanisms Project

The 'BioScholar' system was first described in (Russ et al. 2011) as the first implementation of KEfED. ('KEfED' stands for 'Knowledge Engineering from Experimental Design'). Here, we model the data structure based 'observables' within systems biology studies to develop a library of 'motif's that may be composed together to represent the scientific observations generated within studies of cancer pathways.

This work is funded by DARPA under the Big Mechanism program. The Bioscholar System is developed at the University of Southern California's Information Sciences Institute

Laboratory Notebook for the RUBICON Big Mechanisms Project.

This is concerned with our efforts to apply the KEfED methodology ( http://www.kefed.org/) to the study of cancer pathways as part of the Big Mechanisms DARPA program. Here, we strive to apply this approach to systems biology, making it more agile and effective and supporting multiple activities including text mining and modeling efforts.

This website provides evolving, dynamic technical documentation for KEfED development work supporting various consortia of the DARPA Big Mechanism project.
